We're on a rock floating through space.

That rock is rotating around an axis at ~1000 miles per hour to give us 24 hours in a single day!

So what have I been doing so far with my...

I grew up in a small town on the shoreline of Connecticut known as Old Saybrook.

My sister was and still is one of my best friends, we used to play wiffle ball in our back yard every summer day until the sun went away. Together we never shy away from a challenge, and continue to push each other even today!

After playing a ton of different sports, graduating, and meeting some of the best friends of my life, I shipped out to Massachusetts Maritime Academy. The regimented lifestyle was different but I learned invaluable life lessons, sailed around South America, and played collegiate lacrosse. Nevertheless, I ended up transferring back to CT to go to UConn.

I applied 3 times to get into UConn Storrs and then another 3 times to the Computer Science & Engineering program. After grinding out rigirous academic pre-reqs, the battle of actually getting into the School of Engineering seceded. Fast forward countless trials and tribulations.. My closest friends and I ended up winning UConn Senior design with Hydra (an unfunded, muscle controlled ,3D printed prosthetic arm) and I successfully graduated college with a degree in Computer Science and Engineering.

Shortly after graduating I moved down to RTP in North Carolina. There I met some incredible people, explored the blue mountains, and the ever familiar East coast shoreline. The Atlantic ocean water is a lot nicer than The Sound I was familiar with, the stars seemed brighter too.

A lot of weird nondeterministic events occured for me to end up where I am today, but I couldn't be happier and can't wait to continue pushing myself. I'm currently back up in New England working as a Software Engineer in Providence, RI.

Life is always busy, but I know where home is and I look forward to every day on this life harboring planet hurtling around the universe.
